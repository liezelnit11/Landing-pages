HISTORY

Tanay, a municipality in the province of Rizal, Philippines, has a rich history dating back to the pre-Spanish era when it was inhabited by indigenous tribes. It became an encomienda, or a land grant, in 1583, administered by the Jesuits for the Spanish Crown. Officially founded as a town in 1606, Tanay played a role during the Philippine Revolution against Spain and also during the Philippine-American War. Its geographical location, surrounded by the Sierra Madre mountain range and bordering Laguna de Bay, made it a strategic point of interest throughout its history. Post World War II, Tanay recovered and developed into a vibrant municipality known for its natural attractions, such as Daranak Falls and the Masungi Georeserve, contributing to its local economy through tourism. Its cultural heritage, including the historic Saint Ildefonsus of Toledo Parish Church, reflects its rich history and the blend of influences over centuries. Today, Tanay is a mix of its historical legacy and modern developments, making it an important part of Rizal province's socio-economic and cultural landscape.
